Professional Pothole Repair

Potholes are the result of moisture seeping into pavement areas. When water freezes and thaws repeatedly, it causes the surface to weaken. The weak pavement is then susceptible to erosion from traffic, eventually causing the asphalt and soil beneath to erode away.

Both loose asphalt and potholes are safety hazards that can lead to vehicle damage, falls, and injuries. Florida Sealcoating understands that dealing with potholes around your home or business can be a liability - our team of experts is ready to assist with repairs for residential and commercial properties.

Once a pothole is identified, it's ideal to have it repaired promptly to reduce the potential for further damage and risk of injury.

Our Repair Process

We follow professional techniques to ensure lasting, permanent repairs.

1

Cut & Remove

We saw-cut the damaged area to the sub-base, creating clean edges. This ensures the new asphalt bonds properly to the existing pavement.

2

Base Evaluation

Once the failed asphalt is removed, we evaluate and repair the sub-base as necessary to prevent future failure.

3

Asphalt Installation

We apply asphalt binder and install new SIII hot mix asphalt in layers to the proper depth and grade.

4

Compact & Open

The new asphalt is leveled and compacted with professional equipment. The area can be opened to traffic as soon as the repair is completed.

Types of Asphalt Repairs

We handle all types of asphalt damage, from small potholes to large failing areas:

  • Pothole Repair: Permanent fixes for holes of any size
  • Patching: Repair deteriorated or damaged sections
  • Edge Repair: Fix crumbling edges along curbs and borders
  • Utility Cut Repair: Restore areas after utility work
  • Alligator Cracking: Remove and replace failed areas
  • Depression Repair: Fix low spots that hold water

No job is too big or too small. We do it all with the same attention to quality.

Asphalt Repair FAQs

How much does pothole repair cost in Orlando?
Pothole repair costs depend on size and depth of the damage. We provide free on-site estimates with accurate pricing. Many small repairs are surprisingly affordable - often less than the cost of vehicle damage from hitting the pothole.
How long does asphalt repair take?
Most pothole and patch repairs are completed in a few hours. The area can be opened to traffic immediately after compaction is complete. Larger repairs may take longer depending on the scope.
Can you repair just one pothole?
Absolutely. No job is too small. We handle single pothole repairs as well as large-scale commercial patching projects. We'll assess your situation and recommend the best solution.
Will the repair match my existing asphalt?
New asphalt will initially appear darker than weathered pavement. Over time, the color will blend as the new material oxidizes. We can also follow up with sealcoating to create a uniform appearance.
How can I prevent future potholes?
Regular maintenance is key: seal cracks before water penetrates, sealcoat every 2-4 years to protect the surface, and address drainage issues. Catching problems early prevents expensive repairs later.
