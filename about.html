Evaluate Your Parking Area

Not sure if your asphalt needs attention? Ask yourself these questions to determine if it's time for maintenance or repairs.

Is your asphalt showing signs of fading or graying?
Fading indicates oxidation from UV exposure. This is the first sign that your asphalt's protective oils are breaking down. Sealcoating can restore the rich black color and prevent further deterioration.
Do you see cracks forming in the surface?
Cracks allow water to penetrate beneath the surface, leading to more serious damage over time. Early crack sealing is one of the most cost-effective maintenance steps you can take to extend pavement life.
Are there potholes or areas of significant deterioration?
Potholes create safety hazards and liability concerns. They also indicate underlying structural issues that will only get worse. Professional repair can restore these areas before they spread.
Is water pooling on your pavement after rain?
Standing water indicates drainage issues or surface depressions. This accelerates pavement breakdown and can create hazardous conditions. Proper grading and repairs can resolve these problems.
Are your parking lot lines faded or hard to see?
Faded striping affects traffic flow, parking efficiency, and ADA compliance. Fresh line striping improves safety, maximizes parking capacity, and enhances your property's appearance.
Has it been more than 2-3 years since your last sealcoating?
Regular sealcoating every 2-4 years is essential for protecting your asphalt investment. If it's been a while since your last application, your pavement may be unprotected from Florida's harsh elements.

If you answered yes to any of these questions, it may be time for professional attention.
