Professional Line Striping

Clear, visible parking lot striping is essential for safety, traffic flow, and maximizing your parking capacity. Faded lines lead to confusion, accidents, and liability issues.

Florida Sealcoating provides professional striping services using high-quality traffic paint and precision equipment. Whether you need a complete re-stripe, new layout design, or ADA compliance updates, our experienced team delivers crisp, long-lasting results.

We work efficiently to minimize disruption to your business, often completing projects overnight or during off-hours.

Our Striping Services

We handle all types of pavement marking projects:

  • Parking Stall Striping: Standard spaces, compact spaces, and angled layouts
  • ADA Handicap Spaces: Compliant accessible spaces with proper signage
  • Fire Lanes: Red curb painting and "No Parking - Fire Lane" markings
  • Directional Arrows: Traffic flow arrows and turn indicators
  • Stop Bars & Crosswalks: Pedestrian safety markings
  • Custom Markings: Reserved spaces, loading zones, and specialty markings
  • Warehouse Striping: Safety lanes, forklift paths, and storage areas

ADA Compliance

ADA compliance isn't optional - it's the law. Non-compliant parking lots can result in fines, lawsuits, and barriers for customers with disabilities.

Our team understands current ADA requirements for:

  • Required number of accessible spaces based on lot size
  • Proper space dimensions (standard and van-accessible)
  • Access aisle requirements and markings
  • Signage height and placement
  • Location relative to building entrances

We can evaluate your current layout and recommend changes to bring your parking lot into compliance.

Striping FAQs

How often should parking lot lines be repainted?
Most parking lots need re-striping every 18-24 months, depending on traffic volume and sun exposure. Florida's intense sun can fade lines faster than in northern climates. High-traffic areas may need more frequent touch-ups.
How long does striping take to dry?
Traffic paint typically dries to the touch in 15-30 minutes. We recommend keeping traffic off fresh striping for at least 30 minutes in ideal conditions. Cold or humid conditions may require longer dry times.
Can you stripe at night to avoid business disruption?
Yes, we often complete striping projects during evening hours or overnight to minimize impact on your business operations. We'll work with your schedule to find the best timing.
How many ADA handicap spaces does my lot need?
The number of required accessible spaces depends on your total parking capacity. Generally: 1-25 spaces need 1 accessible space, 26-50 need 2, 51-75 need 3, and so on. One in six accessible spaces must be van-accessible.
Can you redesign my parking lot layout?
Absolutely. We can evaluate your current layout and suggest improvements to maximize capacity, improve traffic flow, or update for ADA compliance. Small changes in layout can sometimes add significant parking capacity.
